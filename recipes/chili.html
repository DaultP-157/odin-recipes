Best Damn Chili

This chili will make your mouth water and it will be your best friend during this winter season

I have made a lot of chili recipes during my days. Some too spicy and some too mild. but this one is just perfect, even my kids who hate chili like to eat this one

Make this beautiful recipe for your friends and family to warm them up this winter season. Once you try it, there's no turning back!

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 Anaheim chile pepper, chopper
  • 2 red jalapeno pepper, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 1/2 pounds lean ground beef
  • 1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 pinch garlic powder, or to taste
  • 2 beef bouillon cubes
  • 1 (12 fluide once) can or bottle light beer (such as coors)
  • 1 (28 once) can crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 (14.5 once) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  • 1 (12 once) can tomatoe paste
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 2 tablesspoons chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon chipotle pepper sauce
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 (16 once) cans dark red kidney beans (such as Bush's)
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin

Steps

  1. Gather all the ingrdients.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat, cook and stir onion, bell pepper, Anaheim pepper, jalapeno peppers and garlic in the hot oil until softened.
  3. Meanwhile, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook and stir beef in the hot skillet until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes.
  4. Add Worcestershire sauce and garlic powder. Crumble bouillon cubes over beef and add beer. Continue to cook, scraping any borwned bits from the bottom of the skillet, until liquid is hot, about 3 minutes.
  5. Stir beef mixture into pepper mixture
  6. Stir crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, and wine to the beef mixture. Season with chili powder, 2 tablespoons cumin, brown sugar, pepper sauce, basil paprika, salt, oregano, and black pepper.
  7. Bring to a boil and reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer until meat and vegetables are very tender and flavors have developed in the chili, about 90 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Mix kidney beans into beef and vegetables. Continue to simmer until beans are hot, about 30 minutes more.
  9. Blend sour cream, cilantro, and remaining 1/2 reaspoon cumin in a food processor until smooth. Serve sour cream mixture with chili.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy
